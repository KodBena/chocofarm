# Synthesis — the formally-grounded controller design space

THE COHERENT PICTURE, GROUNDED IN THE FORMAL MODEL (respecting the caveat: SYNTHESIS.md is an APPROXIMATE forward-derived substrate; its §0 depth==1 / D-dead SPINE is SUPERSEDED in the inquiry regime — chunk_floor ON, depth>1, D LIVE — which I confirmed verbatim against source: runner_wire_batched.hpp:60-119 "D is LIVE only when chunk_floor is on", and runner_wire_batched.cpp:705-749 the gate read ONCE per refill() gating the whole while-burst, the chunk-at-S_min break at :717, the UNGATED forced flush at :748-749. Every model claim leaning on a depth-1 PROOF (§6 boundedness, the §6 mean_rows_per_msg closed form, monotonicity/indexability) is flagged heuristic; the parts I lean on as accurate are the ones the caveat names un-moved: the §2 protocol, §3 timing nondeterminism, the §4 DOF table read FORWARD as a map of knobs the gate reactivates, the §5 regimes, the §6 negative-feedback STRUCTURE.)

1. THE FORMAL OBJECT THE WHOLE DESIGN SPACE COLLAPSES ONTO. Across all three families the formal grounding converges on ONE object: a CONSTRAINED MARKOV DECISION PROCESS on a self-clocking batch-service queue, where the per-thread DENY-only gate is admission control into a single-threaded coalescing server (SYNTHESIS §3.3 "single-thread serialization ... THIS IS THE COALESCING ENGINE"; §6 the negative-feedback batch-size fixed point bounded by the absorbing ceiling min(T·K, max_batch+K−1)). Three sub-structures of this one object recur and organize the families:
  (a) PER-THREAD: each thread is a controlled birth-death chain on the (in-flight-depth j∈{0..D}, un-submitted backlog W) pair — the state variable the model NAMES that v2 only gestured at as loose "backlog". This is the formal home of the static C1 (bang-bang/(s,S) hysteresis), supervised C1/C2 (memoryless logistic / regime-partition tree), online C1/C4 (threshold bandit / SPSA). Reactivated by the caveat: at depth==1 the chain is trivial (j∈{0,1}); chunk_floor un-pins DOF-depth and gives it the multi-state structure the policies need.
  (b) SHARED-RESOURCE COUPLING: the T threads compete for ONE single-threaded server (§3.3 forwards never overlap), so the objective is JOINT — denying thread A changes the value of denying thread B (they coalesce or compete for the same forward window, §5-R1 cross-thread-only batching). This is the structure NO v2 candidate named, and it is the single most consequential thing the formal lens added. It is the formal home of the restless-bandit / Whittle-index formulation (surfaced INDEPENDENTLY by the static AND online families as new candidates), the supervised learning-to-rank / submodular deny-SET (new C6), and the online Lyapunov/backpressure and two-sided team formulations.
  (c) CONSTRAINED-OPTIMIZATION DUAL: the gate enforces a soft coalescing-floor constraint priced by a Lagrange multiplier (online C5 dual ascent, whose setpoint S* IS the code's S_min / DOF-B — controller and plant share one variable). The deepest cross-family structural finding (carried cleanest by the online family) is that THREE apparently unrelated controllers — C5's coalescing-price S*, the Whittle index's per-arm subsidy λ, and the backpressure controller's drift penalty V — are the SAME Lagrangian relaxation of this constrained MDP, "trade queue/coalescing stability for throughput on the §6 self-clocking plant", and §6's own non-divergence proof (the absorbing-ceiling argument) is ALREADY a Lyapunov drift bound at V=0; the controllers add V>0 to trade the model's stability slack for throughput.

Runnable frontier.

The RL-flavored value (temporal credit) is illusory for this horizon-1 self-clocking plant; the ablation will show ≈0 gain. A sharper verdict than v2's "worst sample efficiency".

C3 solves a HARDER (game-theoretic, non-stationary) problem than C1's single-agent bandit; the pool-reward shaping weight β is formally the potential-game construction (Monderer-Shapley) that aligns the Nash with the social optimum.

Finite-MDP exact solvability (value iteration / LP-of-MDP, d'Epenoux) holds but the §6 boundedness that makes the marking finite is proven only for depth-1 (heuristic in depth>1).
   - Cold-compile-shielding guard (static NEW C8): a model-internal contribution, but the 2026-06-19 EMPIRICAL UPDATE REFUTES its premise — the R2 overshoot cold-compile is BENIGN (N=9 emits 56 overshoot compiles yet is the FASTEST case, 143 dps); the stall is the convoy, not the compile. C8 guards a phenomenon the empirics demoted. (The model's own primary characterization "one-time, fully amortized" was right; the redirect that elevated it was refuted.)

3. THE RUNNABLE FRONTIER (what the grounding says to build now, and the single enabling step that unlocks the rest). The model partitions candidates by OBSERVATION, not by family. THREE controllers from three different families — static C2-on-rows/forward, static C5's NUM/Vegas price, static C6's server-place marking (and supervised C6's joint objective benefits too) — ALL bottleneck on ONE enabling step: wiring the server→producer server_rows_per_forward channel (§2.4, the one composition gap on the OBSERVATION side; the server has no producer-facing metrics channel). Until then the model-supported RUNNABLE frontier is exactly the candidates that read only the per-thread (j,W) chain or the live `ready` queue: static C1 + the NEW Whittle/RMAB (per-thread-state-only, multi-thread-coordinated), the NEW Lyapunov/backpressure (ready-only), supervised C1/C2/C4/C5/C6 (offline-labeled, no live server signal needed), online C1/C4/C5. A SECOND, cheaper enabling step (a one-line call-site change at runner:784, the codec exists end-to-end) lights up the rtt channel and unlocks static C5/C8 and the online congestion price.

4. THE TWO-SIDED REFRAME (the model's deepest broadening, beyond a single gate). The online family's NEW two-sided candidate, grounded in the §1 design-intent statement I confirmed verbatim in source (runner_wire_batched.hpp: chunk_floor "ALONE ... floods the single-threaded server with small messages it under-coalesces (the revert's convoy); the design intent is to pair it with the SERVER-side coalescing floor ... the producer supplies the overcommit DEPTH while the server controls the forward WIDTH"), shows the entire v2 issue-gate family is solving a SUB-PROBLEM of a two-actuator TEAM / Stackelberg control problem on the §6 queue: the gate moves DOF-depth/DOF-B (producer depth), but §6 + §3.3 say the throughput-DETERMINING knob is the server drain WIDTH (DOF-F), which the gate cannot touch. This EXPLAINS the recorded producer-floor negative result (commit 3928c21: the gate alone produces the under-coalesced convoy because it cannot move the width knob), and connects the issue-gate controller to the live server-floor-ab work as the TWO HALVES of one team problem to be co-tuned, not designed in isolation.

5. THE DOF TABLE READ FORWARD (the unifying lens the task directs). Every gate the controller actuates moves the DOFs the model PINNED: the gate REACTIVATES DOF-depth/D (the caveat's central un-pinning — j now climbs in {1..D} not identically 1), MOVES DOF-B (coalescing degree, by holding W to fatten the next ⌊W/S_min⌋-chunk burst), nudges DOF-F (self-batching feedback, by shaping which threads' chunks coalesce during a forward, §3.3), and only crosses DOF-S indirectly (service shape, across the max_batch/overshoot boundary). The candidates partition exactly by WHICH DOFs they target: per-thread threshold/hysteresis/bandit → DOF-depth+DOF-B (single-thread); restless-bandit/learning-to-rank/team → the cross-thread DOF-F alignment that §5-R1 says actually sets throughput; the dual/backpressure cluster → DOF-B priced against stability; the refuted cold-compile guard → DOF-S directly (the one candidate acting on the service-time discontinuity, now empirically demoted).

## What the formal grounding changed vs the (unscaffolded) v2 picture

The formal grounding changed the unscaffolded v2 picture in seven structural ways.

(1) IT REVERSED v2's HEADLINE CON FOR EVERY PER-THREAD CONTROLLER. v2's recurring Con — "at the drain-all default depth~1 makes the gate a near-no-op (the forced flush overrides every deny)" (static C1/C3/C4, supervised threshold, online throughout) — is precisely the model's §0 SUPERSEDED SPINE. In the ACTUAL inquiry regime (chunk_floor ON, DOF-depth reactivated, confirmed verbatim in runner_wire_batched.hpp "D is LIVE only when chunk_floor is on"), the gate is the per-thread BURST-DEPTH controller, materially stronger than v2 concedes. v2 was measuring its controllers against the wrong (drain-all) plant; the inquiry regime is the depth>1 plant where the gate has real grip. This single correction re-rates the whole runnable frontier upward.

(2) IT NAMED THE STATE VARIABLE v2 left loose. v2 spoke of "backlog"; the model says the per-thread state is the (in-flight-depth j, un-submitted backlog W) PAIR, and that controllers should read inflight_saturation (j/D) ALONGSIDE ready_backlog_norm (W) — the v2 mechanisms used only W.

(3) IT SURFACED THE SHARED-RESOURCE COUPLING v2 STRUCTURALLY COULD NOT SEE. Every v2 candidate gated threads independently (static C1/C3/C4/C5, online C1-C6) or by an ad-hoc rank (static C2/C6) or via an uncalibrated open-loop net (static C6); NONE recognized the textbook structure "T arms, one activation-constrained shared server" (§3.3). This is why the SAME new candidate — Whittle-index restless-bandit — was surfaced INDEPENDENTLY by both the static and online families, and why the supervised family surfaced its joint-objective analogue (learning-to-rank deny-SET, submodular). The model converted v2's "per-thread credit-assignment confound" (treated as a statistical nuisance) into a SOLVED problem class (RMAB with a shared index dissolves the pool-reward-vs-per-thread-action tension: learned from ONE pooled reward, acts per-thread) and a NAMED game-theoretic fact (price-of-anarchy for the independent-learner C3).

(4) IT UPGRADED "BY ANALOGY" TO "DERIVED". v2 picked memoryless-linear (supervised C1) and bang-bang-over-PID (static C1) by loose analogy; the model DERIVES their well-posedness — §6's negative-feedback fixed point makes the plant a stationary contraction (so a memoryless state→action map is the right class for the R1 modal regime), and §6's monotone plant makes threshold/switching optimal (Stidham-Weber, not "binary actuator ⇒ relay"). It JUSTIFIES parameter-sharing as the §2.2/§2.3 DEALER permutation symmetry (an invariance, not a convenience). Each "derived" claim is flagged heuristic because the witness (§6 monotonicity, the §6 fixed point) is proven only for depth-1.

(5) IT IDENTIFIED THE SENTINEL-0 CHANNELS AS THE PRICE/OUTPUT THE BEST CONTROLLERS NEED — and unified three families on one enabling step. rtt_us = the NUM/Vegas congestion PRICE the §3.3 M/G/1 queue generates (so the RTT-free AIMD is formally underdetermined and the RTT-driven C5 is canonical); server_rows_per_forward = DOF-F = the server-place marking = the rows/FORWARD output. The model showed static C2-on-rows/forward, static C5's price, and static C6's marking ALL bottleneck on wiring the SAME server→producer channel (§2.4) — a convergence v2's analogy-only treatment could not see, and which reframes the design question from "which controller" to "which channel to wire first".

(6) IT REWEIGHTED THE LABEL/OBJECTIVE VIA THE EMPIRICAL CONVOY FINDING. The 2026-06-19 README empirical update (convoy confirmed: §5-R4-like metastable coalescing collapse, B:88→1, ~2-4% of pipelined windows, the stall is the rare non-recovering tail; overshoot REFUTED as benign) reframed the supervised family's binding constraint (the LABEL) as a noisy projection of a BISTABLE feedback variable. Three v2 supervised candidates were re-rated: every observational label is CONFOUNDED by §6 self-recovery (validating the interventional C4 over C1-C3), the dps loss is CONCENTRATED in a measure-small non-convex tail (motivating NEW C5, a calibrated rare-event head — a convex C1 / balanced-label C2 PROVABLY under-serves it), and the objective is JOINT across threads (motivating NEW C6). v2 optimized a balanced "was-the-gate-good" label and spent capacity on the part §6 self-corrects.

(7) IT DEMOTED ONE MODEL-INTERNAL CANDIDATE AND PROMOTED THE TWO-SIDED FRAME. The cold-compile-shielding guard (static NEW C8) was a model contribution the v2 pass could not have named (no concept of the §3.2 compilation discontinuity) — but the SAME 2026-06-19 empirics REFUTE its premise (overshoot is benign; N=9's 56 overshoot compiles are the fastest case), so the grounding both gave and took it away. Conversely the model PROMOTED the issue gate from "the controller" to "one half of a two-actuator team problem" (the §1 producer-depth × server-width co-design), explaining the recorded producer-floor negative result (commit 3928c21) that v2 had no vocabulary for and connecting the gate to the live server-floor-ab work as a single co-tuned object.

NET: v2's analogy-driven picks were not wrong so much as aimed at the wrong plant (drain-all/depth-1) and the wrong target (per-thread-independent, balanced-label). The formal lens re-aimed them at the depth>1 plant and the joint/bistable structure, demoted the two analogies whose required signal is a dead channel (RTT-free AIMD) or whose distinctive value is illusory (RL temporal credit), and added four structurally-motivated candidates v2 missed (Whittle/RMAB twice, learning-to-rank deny-SET, Lyapunov/backpressure, two-sided team) plus the formal reason each fits.

## Open formal questions

- Is the per-thread (j,W) chain INDEXABLE in the depth>1 regime? The Whittle reduction (surfaced by two families) and the threshold-optimality of static C1 both rest on §6's monotone-concave throughput-in-load, which the model PROVES only for the depth-1 fixed point and explicitly does NOT re-derive for depth>1 (the caveat). Indexability can fail (Weber-Weiss); this is the single most load-bearing depth>1 conjecture — falsifiable, and the whole 'principled near-optimal multi-thread gate' claim hangs on it.
- Does the §6 negative-feedback fixed point remain bounded and non-divergent under chunk_floor (depth>1)? §6's absorbing-ceiling proof (two S-independent clamps) was derived for depth-1; under depth>1 the reachable marking set is larger (depth up to D with S_min-quantized jumps) and the model does not re-prove boundedness. The finite-MDP exact-solvability of static C6 (value iteration / LP-of-MDP), the well-posedness of the bandit reward landscape, and the Lyapunov/backpressure drift bound all inherit this gap. Re-deriving the §6 ceiling for depth>1 would convert several heuristic claims to settled.
- WHERE on the §6 batch-size curve does the throughput-maximizing operating point sit, and is the approach monotone or oscillatory? (SYNTHESIS Open Q2: the code leaves S(shape) as bounded nondeterminism, so boundedness is pinned but the interior fixed point's location is not.) This is the surface the online throughput-AIMD / SPSA climbs — the model PROVES it is noisy by construction (S free in a shape band), so a differenced-throughput hill-climber cannot converge tighter than that latitude. The reward-landscape shape (flat saturated plateau in R2 + the now-EMPIRICALLY-DEMOTED overshoot region) determines whether EXP3/discounted-UCB (non-stationary) vs stationary UCB1 is the right bandit, and whether SPSA stalls on a genuinely flat gradient.
- Is the §5-R4 metastable coalescing-collapse basin a depth>1 / DOF-R-reactivated phenomenon? The 2026-06-19 empirics confirm the convoy (B:88→1, ~2-4% of pipelined windows, the non-recovering tail is the stall) but the model's §0 depth-1 spine cannot represent intra-thread reorder (DOF-R vacuous at depth-1). The supervised collapse-classifier (C5) and the whole 'the dps loss lives in the rare tail, not the steady state' framing target a basin whose dynamics the model declines to characterize. A faithful depth>1 model of the collapse trajectory (the README's 'fails to recover and sticks') is the open object the rare-event head is implicitly approximating — and the §6 low-end stickiness was asserted, not derived, per the empirical update.
- What is the price of anarchy of the T-selfish-admission-controller game on the shared §6 server, and what pool-reward shaping weight β makes it a potential game whose Nash coincides with the social optimum? The model NAMES the structure (congested liveness-adjacent Nash, §5-R6 firing probability rising with load) for the independent-learner online C3, but the exact PoA and the β construction (Monderer-Shapley potential) are not quantified — and the gap between C3 (decentralized, congested) and C1 (centralized shared θ, which cannot congest) is itself the substantiation of the parameter-sharing choice.
- Does the two-actuator (producer-gate DOF-B, server-width θ DOF-F) TEAM problem have a clean Stackelberg/team-optimal solution on the §6 queue, and is the gate provably insufficient ALONE? The §1 design-intent (confirmed in source) and the recorded producer-floor negative result (commit 3928c21) strongly suggest the single gate is a follower in a two-player team problem (the throughput-determining knob is the server width the gate cannot move), but the formal team-decomposition (Ho-Chu / bilevel optimization), the leader-commitment value of θ, and the co-tuning of the issue-gate with the live server-floor-ab controller are unworked — this is the broadening the lens opened but did not close.
- Once the server→producer server_rows_per_forward channel (§2.4 observation gap) is wired, do static C2-on-rows/forward and static C6 (now closed-loop on DOF-F) actually CONVERGE, and is the resulting regulator equivalent to the dual C5-on-rows/forward? The model predicts all three collapse onto the same DOF-F regulator once the one observable exists, but the equivalence is asserted from the reductions, not proven — and it is the single highest-leverage enabling step, so whether the three families' candidates literally unify there is worth pinning.
