# Phase 3 — Design exploration: static-control

**Frame:** Frame: the plant is a single fixed-cost batched server fed by T producer threads, each holding up to D coalesced messages in flight over its own DEALER socket. The control task is to keep the server's batches full (amortize the fixed per-forward cost) AND keep the depth-1 producers from blocking idle on a single outstanding reply, WITHOUT producer-RSS runaway. The empirical result (server-gen-floor-result.md, read end to end) reframes the objective sharply: the win is NOT raw mean dps (the gen-floor's min only ties greedy's max; +3.7% mean) — it is VARIANCE: removing the metastable-collapse tail ([169-183] -> [183-184]), and the mechanism is producer-idle-time reduction (depth>1 keeps depth-1 threads from blocking), NOT bigger server batches (the server already reaches ~195 rows/fwd greedily; the winning gen-floor runs at SMALLER 108 rows/fwd). This inverts the naive control objective: the controller is a TAIL/VARIANCE regulator, not a throughput maximizer, and its real lever is per-thread occupancy/idle-avoidance, not server-batch-width maximization.

Two hard constraints shape every candidate. (1) The primitive action is a per-thread BINARY issue gate (may_issue in {allow,deny}), consulted once per refill; the controller never sets D (the runner's fixed safety ceiling) and overcommit (depth>1) is a DOWNSTREAM emergent property of allowed-issues x in-flight history, not the action (ADR-0008). So a controller raises emergent depth by ALLOWING broadly and lowers it by DENYING — a fractional in-flight depth is realized as a duty cycle across threads x time, which is a coarse, lagged, integral-only actuator (you cannot push depth up directly; you can only stop denying and let replies refill). (2) The control tick is SLOW (cadence_ms) and reads marshalled IssueFeatures; the hot gate read is one relaxed atomic; the policy must be cheap (an O(T) reduction, T~3-4). The forced-flush floor + D ceiling already provide liveness+safety underneath ANY policy (a denied thread still drains at depth-1 via the ungated forced flush, and inflight can never exceed D), so the policy's stability burden is bounded: its worst failure is suboptimal throughput, never a deadlock or an RSS blowout past D.

A structural subtlety that constrains observability: publish() fires AFTER refill() (runner line 780-784), so the inflight the policy sees on a tick is the POST-refill (topped-up) depth, not the pre-reply trough. The momentary idle a thread suffers between emptying its pipe and the reply landing is NOT directly observable on the tick — the policy must INFER incipient idleness from level+rate signals (low fill, falling issue_rate, ready piling up), or from RTT once that field is wired. This is why a pure reactive bang-bang on instantaneous inflight is weak: the signal it would react to is already smoothed by the refill that precedes the sample. It argues for either (a) an integral/duty-cycle controller that targets a sustained aggregate fill rather than chasing the instantaneous level, or (b) wiring the trivial-to-add RTT field so latency carries the idle signal the inflight level hides.

Given all this, the design space splits along an axis I use to organize the candidates: AGGREGATE-vs-PER-THREAD altitude (one server => an aggregate fill target is arguably the right altitude; but the action is per-thread and the idle is per-thread), and REACTIVE-vs-INTEGRAL actuation (instantaneous threshold/hysteresis vs duty-cycle/token-bucket that holds a steady emergent depth). Because the action can only INCREASE depth by NOT denying, the most natural framings are admission-control / backpressure ones where allow is the default and deny is a brake applied only against an RSS/saturation signal — the opposite of a throughput controller that must actively push. I evaluate eight candidates spanning hysteresis/bang-bang, AIMD, token/leaky-bucket admission, PI on a fill setpoint, backpressure/Lyapunov, queueing-network (Little's-law) admission, MPC, and a defer-to-RL identity/affine baseline.

## Candidates

### C1 — Per-thread hysteresis (Schmitt-trigger bang-bang on inflight_fill)
- **maps features to gate:** Per thread t: allow[t] = (fill[t] < f_hi) where fill[t]=inflight[t]/d_ceiling, with a two-threshold hysteresis band: once denied (fill crossed f_hi), stay denied until fill drops below f_lo (<f_hi); once allowing, stay allowing until fill exceeds f_hi. Requires one bit of controller-side state per thread (the current gate latch). The convoy_score (ready_pressure*(1-fill)) can OVERRIDE to force allow when starvation is forming regardless of the latch.
- **objective or signal:** Setpoint is an inflight-fill band [f_lo, f_hi] (e.g. [0.4, 0.85] of D). The band, not a point, is the whole idea: avoid chattering the gate at the boundary, which itself would induce the convoy the policy exists to prevent (an oscillating allow pattern modulates emergent depth at the worst frequency).
- **tuning or training:** Offline grid/Sobol over (f_lo, f_hi) on the wire_ab_bench warm-pool sweep (--pool-seed) at S_min=32/D in {16,32}; pick the (f_lo,f_hi) minimizing the dps MIN-over-seeds (the variance bar, ADR-0009) not the mean. ~4-6 points each axis.
- **stability and liveness:** Hysteresis guarantees no limit-cycle chatter (the band sets a minimum dwell). Liveness is free: a denied thread drains at depth-1 via the ungated forced flush; D bounds RSS. BIBO-stable trivially (bounded relay output, bounded state). No windup (no integrator).
- **inference cost:** O(T) compares + one latch bit per thread. Negligible. No history beyond the latch.
- **pros:** Dead simple, O(T), no model, no training; the hysteresis band is the textbook cure for the relay-chatter instability that a single-threshold bang-bang would suffer on a noisy post-refill sample; directly encodes the contract's 'allow when about to idle (low fill), deny when saturated (high fill)'; the convoy_score override gives it the one pre-emptive degree of freedom the variance objective needs. Cheapest possible thing that is not identity.
- **cons:** Reacts to the post-refill (smoothed) fill, so it is blind to the pre-reply idle trough — its allow at low fill may already be late. f_hi/f_lo are two magic numbers per regime; the doc shows the operating point is D-insensitive once D>=16 so the FRACTION should transfer, but f_hi/f_lo still need a sweep. Purely per-thread: cannot see that the aggregate server is starved while each thread looks individually healthy.
- **adr fit:** Excellent. P2: pure function of IssueFeatures behind IssuePolicy. P4: f_lo/f_hi are live tunables (ctor args / swept), not frozen invariants. P5: leans entirely on the existing forced-flush+D liveness floor. P8/P9: trivial typed signature. ADR-0008: action stays 'issue allow/deny'; the latch is controller-internal, never named overcommit.
- **risks:** If the post-refill smoothing hides the idle entirely, C1 degenerates toward identity (always allow, since fill rarely reaches f_hi at depth~1). Mitigated only if the regime actually drives fill high enough to bite — needs the empirical check that fill[t] has usable dynamic range on the tick.

### C2 — AIMD on aggregate target depth, realized as a per-thread allow duty cycle
- **maps features to gate:** Maintain a single controller-side scalar D_target (a fractional aggregate in-flight target, real-valued in [T, T*D]). Each tick: if a CONGESTION signal fires (rising rtt-with-ready, or convoy_score over threshold, or sum_ready climbing) multiplicatively decrease D_target *= beta (e.g. 0.7); else additively increase D_target += alpha (e.g. +0.5). Then realize D_target as allow bits: rank threads by fill and ALLOW the lowest-fill threads until expected aggregate inflight reaches D_target, DENY the rest. allow_history/deny_frac (the integral) closes the loop so the realized aggregate tracks D_target rather than chattering.
- **objective or signal:** Congestion signal = the convoy precursor (high sum_ready AND/OR rising rtt). AIMD's setpoint is implicit: it probes upward (more allowed => more emergent depth => server better fed) until it sees congestion, then backs off — the classic TCP-style 'fill the pipe, back off at loss' shaped for this plant.
- **tuning or training:** Offline-tune (alpha, beta, congestion-threshold) on the warm-pool sweep; or self-tune online (AIMD is designed to be parameter-robust — alpha/beta near TCP's 1/0.5 transfer broadly). The congestion threshold is the one regime-sensitive knob; calibrate it against the doc's collapse floor (the 169 lows).
- **stability and liveness:** AIMD is provably fair+stable (converges to a fixed point under the standard fluid model) and the multiplicative decrease bounds excursions; combined with the hard D ceiling and forced-flush floor it cannot run away. The realized-vs-target gap (deny_frac integral) prevents windup. Main stability risk is coupling between the slow tick and RTT — keep cadence_ms >> mean RTT.
- **inference cost:** O(T log T) for the fill-rank (T~3-4 => trivial) or O(T) with a threshold instead of a rank; one scalar update; a per-thread deny_frac EWMA. Cheap.
- **pros:** AIMD is the proven distributed-congestion control for exactly 'keep a shared resource fed without overrunning it'; its sawtooth naturally finds the largest safe emergent depth and the multiplicative-decrease gives a fast escape from a forming convoy (the variance tail). The aggregate target is the RIGHT ALTITUDE for the single server (the contract's own 'per-thread gating aggregated over threads x time emulates a dynamic fractional in-flight depth' is literally AIMD's duty cycle). deny_frac as the integral term is the anti-windup/anti-chatter the surface already names.
- **cons:** AIMD needs a clean binary CONGESTION signal; the cleanest one (rtt-with-ready) is gated on the trivial_to_add RTT field being wired (sentinel today). Without RTT it must lean on convoy_score/sum_ready, which are noisier proxies. The additive-increase probe deliberately overshoots into mild congestion to find the edge — that periodic probe-induced congestion is in tension with a pure VARIANCE objective (it manufactures small tail events to locate the boundary). Sawtooth period must be slow relative to RTT or it fights the pipe.
- **adr fit:** Strong. P2 seam-clean (the AIMD scalar is controller-internal state). P4: alpha/beta/threshold live. ADR-0008: the action remains per-thread issue allow/deny; D_target is an emergent-depth TARGET the policy holds via the duty cycle, never an action and never conflated with the runner's D. P5: rides the liveness floor. ADR-0009: the sawtooth's win must be measured min-over-seeds.
- **risks:** Best congestion signal needs RTT wired first. If only convoy_score is available the decrease may trigger late (convoy_score is a level, not a leading edge). The deliberate probe-overshoot may itself add tail variance — needs the measured check that net variance drops.

### C3 — Token-bucket / leaky-bucket issue admission (rate-shaped allow)
- **maps features to gate:** A token bucket per thread (or one shared aggregate bucket) refilled at rate r tokens/tick, capacity C. A thread is ALLOWED iff its bucket has >=1 token; consuming an allow spends a token (charged via the issue_rate the thread realizes between ticks — Delta msgs[t] tokens spent). DENY when the bucket is empty. The refill rate r is the control: set r from a setpoint on aggregate fill or server-fed proxy (raise r when sum_inflight is low / server under-fed, lower r when RSS pressure rises).
- **objective or signal:** Setpoint = a target sustained ISSUE RATE (messages/s) that keeps the server fed without RSS runaway — the leaky-bucket smooths bursty issuing into a steady egress, which is exactly what a single-threaded server amortizing a fixed cost wants (steady arrival => steady full forwards, low variance). The bucket's capacity C bounds burstiness (the RSS guard).
- **tuning or training:** Offline-tune (r setpoint mapping, capacity C) on the sweep; C is sized from the D ceiling and the measured RSS-per-message so the burst bound maps to the 6 GiB kill-ceiling margin the doc records.
- **stability and liveness:** Leaky-bucket is unconditionally stable (bounded output rate r, bounded backlog C by construction). Liveness floor intact (an empty bucket => deny => depth-1 forced flush). The bucket cannot windup (capacity-clamped). The only instability is in the OUTER loop that sets r, inheriting whichever scheme (C2/C4) drives it.
- **inference cost:** O(T): one token update + compare per thread. Plus the issue_rate differencing (a subtract per thread). Cheap.
- **pros:** Token/leaky-bucket is THE canonical admission-control primitive for shaping a bursty source into a smooth feed of a fixed-rate server; capacity C is a direct, interpretable RSS-burst bound (cleaner than a soft fill threshold). It converts the binary gate into a rate controller without any per-tick model. Naturally rate-based, so it composes with the issue_rate derived feature the surface already wants. Smoothing bursts is plausibly the most direct attack on the convoy (a convoy is a burst-then-starve oscillation).
- **cons:** Charging tokens requires the issue_rate differencing (controller-side history, msgs[t] differenced over the tick) — fine, but it means the bucket is updated on the SLOW tick, so the shaping granularity is cadence_ms, coarse relative to the issue dynamics. Setting r still needs a setpoint feeding back from fill/server-fed — so C3 is really a rate-INNER-loop that needs C2-or-C4 as its outer loop; alone it shapes burstiness but doesn't choose the operating point. Per-thread buckets can desync (one thread hogs); a shared bucket loses per-thread idle targeting.
- **adr fit:** Good, with a caveat: as a standalone it under-determines the operating point, so honest framing per ADR-0008 is 'a rate-shaping inner loop', not a complete policy. P2/P4/P5 all clean (bucket state controller-side, r live, liveness floor underneath). The capacity-as-RSS-bound is a nice P5 'remove-the-root-cause' framing (the burst, not a band-aid on the symptom).
- **risks:** Incomplete alone (needs an outer setpoint loop). Slow-tick token granularity may be too coarse to shape the actual burst dynamics that drive the convoy — the convoy may form and clear faster than cadence_ms.

### C4 — PI controller on an aggregate fill setpoint (allow as the quantized actuator)
- **maps features to gate:** Error e = fill_setpoint - mean_fill (mean_fill = sum_inflight/(T*d_ceiling)). u = Kp*e + Ki*integral(e). Map the continuous u to the binary gate by a duty-cycle quantizer: ALLOW the round(u-scaled fraction * T) lowest-fill threads, DENY the rest (or per-thread: allow[t] = (fill[t] < setpoint + Kp*e_local)). The integral term IS the deny_frac/allow_history state, giving zero steady-state error in the emergent depth.
- **objective or signal:** An explicit aggregate fill SETPOINT (e.g. mean_fill = 0.6 of D) chosen to sit at the empirical peak (the doc's S_min=32/D=16 operating point reached emergently). PI drives the realized emergent depth to that setpoint and HOLDS it (integral kills steady-state error), which is precisely a variance-minimizing regulator: it pins the operating point at the top of greedy's range rather than letting it wander into the 169 lows.
- **tuning or training:** Offline: identify the plant gain+lag from logged (allow-fraction, emergent-depth) traces (the realized_inflight_depth_trajectory label), then Ziegler-Nichols / lambda-tuning for Kp/Ki; OR auto-tune via a relay-feedback experiment in the harness. Setpoint = the empirical peak fill. ADR-0009: validate the held-variance min-over-seeds.
- **stability and liveness:** Stable IF tuned for the transport lag (gain/phase margin); the lag is real (the realized_inflight_depth_trajectory shows depth responds several ticks after an allow change) so conservative Kp/Ki + a slow cadence are required. Anti-windup mandatory (clamp the integral when the gate saturates all-allow or all-deny, which it will at the extremes). Liveness floor + D ceiling bound the worst case regardless of tuning.
- **inference cost:** O(T) reduction + a couple FLOPs for the PI update + the duty-cycle quantizer. Cheap. History = one integral accumulator + the prior allow vector.
- **pros:** A regulator that HOLDS a setpoint is the most direct expression of the variance objective (the doc literally wants [183-184] held, not a mean chased). PI is the standard, well-understood tool; the integral term gives zero offset in emergent depth and is the natural home for the allow_history/deny_frac the surface already identifies as the closed-loop necessity. Aggregate altitude matches the single server. Lead/derivative term optional (D on rising sum_ready => pre-empt the convoy = the variance tail), making it PID if the rate signals prove useful.
- **cons:** Binary actuation of a continuous u is a quantization (duty-cycle or per-thread threshold) — introduces a limit-cycle risk that needs either dithering or a deadband. Kp/Ki need tuning and the plant gain (allow-fraction -> emergent-depth) is nonlinear and LAGGED (depth responds several RTTs after the allow change, since it builds up through refills) — the lag is the classic PI-destabilizer; cadence_ms must be slow enough that the loop sees the settled response. Needs the SETPOINT chosen well (offline, from the sweep's peak).
- **adr fit:** Strong, modulo the honesty that the actuator is quantized (name it: 'PI with duty-cycle quantization', not 'PI control', per ADR-0008 precision). P2/P4 clean (gains live, setpoint live). P5 liveness floor. ADR-0009: the lag-identification is itself a substantiated-claim obligation (log the trajectory, fit the gain).
- **risks:** The allow->depth lag is the central risk; an under-damped PI will oscillate the emergent depth at exactly the convoy frequency — the cure (slow cadence, conservative gains, anti-windup) may make it sluggish enough to barely beat hysteresis. Quantization limit-cycles. Needs the realized-depth trajectory logged to identify the plant at all.

### C5 — Backpressure / Lyapunov-drift admission (deny against a virtual queue)
- **maps features to gate:** Model a virtual RSS/backlog queue Q (e.g. Q = sum_ready or sum_inflight beyond a target, the rss_pressure_proxy). Per-thread Lyapunov-drift-plus-penalty rule: ALLOW thread t iff V*utility(issue) - Q*cost(issue) > 0, i.e. allow when the throughput utility of issuing outweighs the queue (RSS/convoy) pressure it adds; DENY otherwise. V trades off throughput vs backlog. Concretely: allow[t] = (V*ready_pressure[t] > Q_agg) — allow the threads with the most parked work to feed (highest marginal server-feed utility) until the aggregate backlog penalty dominates.
- **objective or signal:** Maximize a throughput utility (server-fed proxy / issue_rate) MINUS V-weighted queue (RSS/backlog) drift. The Lyapunov function is the backlog; minimizing its drift-plus-penalty is provably throughput-optimal-up-to-O(1/V) while keeping the queue O(V)-bounded — exactly the 'maximize sustained throughput without producer-RSS runaway' objective stated as a constrained optimization.
- **tuning or training:** One knob V (the throughput-vs-backlog weight); sweep it offline to trace the frontier and pick the V whose operating point matches the empirical peak with minimal variance. utility/cost scaling identified from logged traces. Genuinely close to self-tuning (V is the only real DOF).
- **stability and liveness:** Lyapunov drift is the STRONGEST stability story here: the drift-plus-penalty rule provably keeps the virtual queue mean-rate-stable (bounded backlog) by construction — it is a stability proof, not just a stable controller. Liveness floor + D ceiling are redundant safety beneath a scheme that already bounds the queue. No windup (greedy, stateless-per-tick except the queue, which is the plant's own integral).
- **inference cost:** O(T): one utility-minus-penalty compare per thread + the aggregate queue update. Cheap. History = the virtual queue scalar(s).
- **pros:** This is the textbook-correct formulation of THE stated objective (max throughput s.t. bounded backlog) — Neely-style drift-plus-penalty gives a PROVABLE throughput-vs-backlog tradeoff with a single tunable V, and the bound on Q is the rigorous version of the RSS guard. It is greedy-per-tick (no setpoint to mistune), naturally per-thread (allow the highest-marginal-utility threads), and the [O(1/V), O(V)] tradeoff curve is exactly the variance-vs-throughput frontier the doc maps empirically. Strong fit to the project's taste for provable bounds (the dual-bound culture).
- **cons:** Requires defining utility and cost in commensurable units, and the throughput-utility's best proxy (server-fed) is the in-process SURROGATE (server_rows_per_forward is the gap-channel quantity) — so the optimality proof is against a proxy, an honesty caveat. The virtual queue Q must be constructed from in-process signals (sum_ready / rss_pressure), not a real RSS read (RSS isn't in IssueFeatures). V tunes the operating point; the per-tick greedy can be myopic if the utility/cost are mis-scaled.
- **adr fit:** Excellent and idiomatic. Matches the project's provable-bound culture; P2/P4/P5 clean; the queue-bound is a P5 'remove-the-root-cause' (bound the backlog, don't band-aid the symptom). ADR-0008: per-thread issue allow/deny, the queue is an emergent-backlog observation not an action. ADR-0009: the O(1/V)/O(V) tradeoff is a SUBSTANTIATABLE claim — measure the frontier.
- **risks:** The optimality is against the in-process server-fed SURROGATE, not the true server rows/forward (gap_needs_channel) — so the proof's premise is approximate until the redis-key side-channel or P7 reply-field delivers the real metric. Mis-scaled utility/cost units could make the greedy myopic. Most conceptual setup of the candidates.

### C6 — Queueing-network admission via Little's law (target a WIP that fills forwards)
- **maps features to gate:** Treat the producer->server->reply as a closed queueing network; by Little's law the in-flight WIP that sustains a target server occupancy is L = lambda_target * W (W = mean RTT, lambda_target = the issue rate that keeps forwards full). Compute a target aggregate inflight L* from the measured RTT and the desired throughput, then ALLOW threads until sum_inflight reaches L*, DENY beyond. CONWIP-style: a fixed WIP cap realized as the allow gate, with L* set so the server's batch stays full.
- **objective or signal:** Setpoint = a WIP level L* derived from Little's law to keep the server's forward at its amortization knee (enough rows/forward to amortize the fixed cost, not more). This directly targets the 'keep batches full' lever via the one law that connects in-flight depth, throughput, and latency.
- **tuning or training:** Near-self-tuning: L* is computed, not tuned; the only knobs are the RTT EWMA alpha and the lambda_target estimator. Validate against the sweep that the computed L* lands at the empirical peak.
- **stability and liveness:** CONWIP (a fixed WIP cap) is unconditionally stable (bounded WIP by construction = the RSS bound). Liveness floor intact. The adaptivity comes from L* tracking W; as long as the W estimate is smoothed (EWMA) the L* update is stable. Risk: a noisy RTT estimate makes L* jitter — needs the EWMA/trend (rtt_load) smoothing.
- **inference cost:** O(T): the WIP compare per thread + the L* scalar update (one multiply). Cheap. History = the RTT EWMA + lambda estimate.
- **pros:** Little's law is the exact analytic bridge between the in-flight DEPTH the controller modulates and the THROUGHPUT it wants — it turns 'how much overcommit do I need' into a computed L* rather than a swept threshold, and it self-adapts as RTT changes (more server load => larger W => larger L* automatically => allow more depth, which is the CORRECT response per the doc: rising rtt under low ready means the server is the bottleneck, keep depth). CONWIP is a proven, robust, low-parameter shop-floor control. Self-tuning once RTT is wired.
- **cons:** L = lambda*W REQUIRES W (mean RTT) — gated entirely on the trivial_to_add RTT field (sentinel today); without RTT the law has no input and C6 is inert. lambda_target (the throughput goal) is itself what we're trying to maximize, so it must be estimated (e.g. the recent best issue_rate) — a mild circularity. Assumes a steady-state queueing regime; the convoy is precisely a NON-steady metastable transient, so the law describes the good regime but not the collapse it must prevent (it keeps you in the good regime rather than escaping a bad one).
- **adr fit:** Good. Analytic, low-parameter, self-adapting — idiomatic for the project. P2/P4/P5 clean. ADR-0008: WIP cap realized as per-thread allow; depth is the emergent quantity the law TARGETS, named honestly. ADR-0009: the Little's-law derivation is a checkable model (validate L* against measured rows/forward).
- **risks:** Hard dependency on the RTT field (inert without it). The steady-state-queueing premise does not cover the metastable convoy transient — C6 prevents drift but may not escape an already-forming collapse as fast as C2's multiplicative decrease. Circular lambda_target estimate.

### C7 — Model-Predictive Control over a short horizon (rollout the emergent depth)
- **maps features to gate:** A small predictive model of the plant (allow-fraction -> emergent inflight depth -> windowed dps), identified offline from the logged trajectories (realized_inflight_depth_trajectory + future_window_dps). Each tick, optimize the per-thread allow vector over a short horizon H (a few ticks) to maximize predicted windowed dps minus an RSS-overshoot penalty, apply the first step's allow bits (receding horizon).
- **objective or signal:** Directly the offline objective: predicted future_window_dps over horizon H, penalized for predicted RSS/backlog overshoot — the supervised labels (future_window_dps, collapse_onset, realized depth trajectory) ARE the MPC's plant model and cost.
- **tuning or training:** Offline system-ID of the plant model from logged paired traces (--pool-seed for counterfactual_allow_delta); horizon H and the cost weights tuned offline. The most data-hungry (needs many warm-pool-seeded runs covering collapse + non-collapse windows — the rare-positive-class problem the offline-label section flags).
- **stability and liveness:** MPC stability needs a terminal cost/constraint or a sufficiently long horizon; with a short H and a crude model, stability is NOT guaranteed analytically — it leans on the forced-flush+D floor as the ultimate backstop (which it has). Recursive feasibility is easy here because all-allow is always feasible (the identity baseline). Liveness floor intact.
- **inference cost:** Highest: a model forward + an 8^H (or beam) search per tick. Tractable only because T and H are tiny and the cadence is slow; still the one candidate where 'cheap on the control tick' is a real design constraint to verify.
- **pros:** The only candidate that can ANTICIPATE the lagged allow->depth->dps response (the lag that destabilizes the PI and that the post-refill sampling hides) — MPC's whole point is handling dead-time/lag by predicting forward. With a collapse-onset-aware cost it can pre-emptively allow before the convoy, the exact variance-tail attack. Can incorporate the RSS constraint as a hard constraint (the cleanest expression of 'no RSS runaway').
- **cons:** By far the heaviest: needs an identified plant model (offline system-ID from the logged trajectories), a per-tick optimization (even over a tiny binary action space 2^T with T~3-4 = 8-16 candidates per step, H steps => manageable but it IS an inner solve), and is the most likely to overfit a regime (the model is only valid where it was identified — regime_annotation matters). The control tick must stay cheap; an 8^H enumeration is fine at T=3,H=2 but the model eval cost is the question. Borders on 'not a STATIC policy' (the brief asks for literature/engineering-informed STATIC policies) — the model is trained offline but the per-tick optimize is a solve.
- **adr fit:** Weakest fit to the 'static policy' framing (it is model-based, model identified offline but optimizing online) — name that honestly per ADR-0008. P2 clean (model behind IssuePolicy). ADR-0009: the plant model is a substantiated claim (cross-validate per regime). It edges toward the RL/learned-policy territory the brief separates from static control.
- **risks:** Overfit to one operating point (regime brittleness); model-mismatch instability; the per-tick solve cost; and the framing risk that it is not really a 'static control-theoretic' policy but a learned model in disguise. Best held as the upper-bound reference the simpler controllers are measured against, not the first thing to ship.

### C8 — Identity / static-affine baseline (the regression anchor)
- **maps features to gate:** allow[t] = 1 for all t (identity — byte-unchanged vs no controller, the seam's documented default). Or a one-line static-affine variant: allow[t] = (fill[t] < c) for a single fixed constant c (a plain one-threshold gate, no hysteresis, no state).
- **objective or signal:** None / the trivial setpoint. Its job is to be the REGRESSION BASELINE (the way RandomPolicy validates the env/Policy seam): any nontrivial candidate must beat identity's variance min-over-seeds to justify itself (ADR-0009).
- **tuning or training:** None (identity) or one constant c (affine).
- **stability and liveness:** Trivially stable; identical to the existing validated runner. Liveness/safety are the runner's own.
- **inference cost:** Zero (identity) / O(T) compare (affine).
- **pros:** Zero risk, zero cost, zero state; proves the seam end-to-end (a real policy plugs in and the run still drains correctly); the mandatory control in any A/B (the doc's whole method is A/B against greedy). The single-threshold variant is the ablation that isolates whether hysteresis (C1) actually buys anything over a bare threshold.
- **cons:** Identity does nothing for the convoy (it IS the depth-1 greedy that exhibits the [169-183] swing the gen-floor exists to fix — though note: under a controller with chunk_floor OFF the runner is depth-1 anyway, so identity here reproduces greedy). The single-threshold variant chatters (no hysteresis) — that chatter is itself informative (it demonstrates why C1's band is needed).
- **adr fit:** Perfect — it is the seam's documented default and the ADR-0009 regression anchor. Every other candidate is measured against it.
- **risks:** None as a baseline; the risk is mistaking it for a solution (it is the null hypothesis the variance result already characterizes).

## Recommendation
Try first, in order: C8 (identity + single-threshold affine) as the mandatory regression anchor — the seam already defaults to it, so it costs nothing and it is the A/B control the whole method (and ADR-0009) requires; then C1 (per-thread hysteresis with a convoy_score override) as the simplest thing that could actually move the VARIANCE needle, because the objective is explicitly tail/variance reduction and hysteresis is the textbook cure for the relay-chatter that an oscillating allow pattern would itself induce. C1 needs no new channel (it runs on the already-wired inflight/ready/d_ceiling) and its convoy_score override is the one pre-emptive DOF the variance tail needs.

In parallel, wire the trivial-to-add RTT field (producer-local, no cross-process channel — a corr-id->submit-mono_ns store in the WireLeafPool path, EWMA'd at recv), because it unlocks the two strongest candidates: C5 (Lyapunov drift-plus-penalty) and C6 (Little's-law CONWIP). C5 is the recommendation for the SECOND wave: it is the textbook-correct statement of the literal objective (maximize throughput subject to bounded backlog), it has a single tunable V tracing exactly the throughput-vs-variance frontier the doc maps empirically, its queue-bound is a provable RSS guard idiomatic to the project's dual-bound culture, and it is per-thread and greedy (no setpoint to mistune, no lag-induced PI oscillation). Its one honesty caveat — the throughput utility uses the in-process server-fed SURROGATE because the true server_rows_per_forward is the gap-channel quantity — is exactly why the offline labels (regime_annotation carrying SERVER_STATS mean_real_rows_per_fwd) matter: distill the gap-channel signal offline so the deployable in-process C5 approximates it.

Hold C2 (AIMD) as the strong alternative if a clean leading-edge congestion signal (rtt-with-ready) proves available — it gives the fastest convoy ESCAPE (multiplicative decrease) where C5/C6 prevent-but-may-not-escape. Hold C7 (MPC) as the offline UPPER-BOUND reference the simple controllers are scored against, not a first ship — it borders on a learned policy and risks regime overfit. C3 (token-bucket) and C4 (PI) are valuable as COMPONENTS (C3 the rate-shaping inner loop, C4 if a held-setpoint regulator is wanted) but each is incomplete or lag-fragile alone. Decisive empirical pre-check before investing in any controller beyond C1: confirm on a logged run that inflight_fill has usable dynamic range ON THE POST-REFILL TICK (publish fires after refill, runner line 780-784) — if the post-refill smoothing flattens fill near depth-1, the level-based candidates (C1/C4) degenerate toward identity and the RTT-based ones (C5/C6/C2) become mandatory, because latency is then the only observable that still carries the idle signal.

## What the fixture must provide
For C8/C1 (no new channel): nothing beyond what is wired — inflight[], ready[], d_ceiling, n_threads are live. C1 additionally needs the controller to retain ONE latch bit per thread (the hysteresis state) and to compute convoy_score = ready_pressure*(1-fill) from the per-thread vectors (an O(T) reduction in tick_). S_target (for ready_pressure normalization) must be handed to the controller as a run constant (it already knows D; S_min/chunk_floor live in the same WireRunnerConfig — pass them through the IssueController ctor alongside d_ceiling, the same read-only-feature pattern).

For the rate/integral candidates (C2/C3/C4): the controller must retain HISTORY tick_ does not hold today — prev_msgs[]/prev_leaves[], a steady_clock tick timestamp (for issue_rate = Delta msgs / dt), and the prior allow vector (for deny_frac/allow_history, the integral term). These are controller-side derivations, NOT producer publishes; the surface explicitly warns rates do not arrive pre-differenced. No producer change. Add steady_clock::now() capture in tick_.

For the latency-aware candidates (C2 congestion-signal, C5 utility, C6 Little's-law): WIRE THE RTT FIELD — the one trivial_to_add producer change. WireLeafPool::inflight_ is unordered_map<uint64_t, vector<int>> (slots only, no time — verified at wire_leaf_pool.hpp:147,242); add a parallel corr-id->submit-mono_ns store (or a second map field), record now at issue()/submit_batch, subtract at recv_batch resolution (runner line 763-765), EWMA into a per-thread my_rtt, and PASS IT as publish()'s 6th arg (currently defaulted 0.0 and never passed at runner line 784). Purely producer-local, no cross-process channel. C6 cannot run at all without this (Little's law needs W); C5/C2 run on proxies without it but are sharply better with it.

For C5/C7 optimality and any offline tuning/validation (ADR-0009): the eval harness is wire_ab_bench's reproducible warm-pool sweep (--pool-seed) — use it as the matched-pair generator. Log per control tick: the full IssueFeatures snapshot + the written allow vector + a steady_clock timestamp + a timestamped total_decisions counter (total_decisions is already maintained in the runner; emit it with a timestamp each tick for the future_window_dps label) + the run's WireRunnerConfig (regime_annotation) + the pool-seed (so counterfactual pairs are joinable). The end-of-run SERVER_STATS mean_real_rows_per_fwd (printed at subprocess exit, stage_a_server.py:182) is the offline GROUND-TRUTH the in-process server-fed surrogate approximates — log it per run and join by run id so an offline model (or C5's utility calibration) can be trained against the TRUE server rows/forward even though the deployed policy runs on the surrogate.

The TRUE server_rows_per_forward as a LIVE feature (would benefit C5/C6 directly, removing the surrogate caveat) remains gap_needs_channel — it has no live carrier (the reply frame [ident,*envelope,resp] is a pure value codec; verified). Lowest-touch live channel is a redis-key the server writes (it already has redis) and the controller reads on its SLOW tick — no P7 codec change, off the hot path. This is OPTIONAL for shipping (the surrogate + offline-distillation path works without it) but is the clean upgrade if a latency/variance gap remains that the surrogate cannot close. Do NOT extend the reply codec for it (a P7 cross-language-wire change for a slow-cadence scalar is disproportionate; the side-channel is the proportionate mechanism).

## Key uncertainties
(1) Dynamic range of the observable on the post-refill tick. publish() fires AFTER refill() (runner 780-784), so the inflight the policy reads is already topped back toward D — the pre-reply idle TROUGH is invisible. If, with chunk_floor OFF, the runner is structurally depth~1 (SYNTHESIS §0: drain-all => a second issue finds nothing ready => depth identically 1, D dead), then inflight is ~1 on every tick and inflight_fill has NO range for a level-based policy to act on. This is the single biggest uncertainty: it may mean the controller can ONLY meaningfully act when chunk_floor is ON (depth>1 exists to modulate), OR that the controller is the depth-1 path's ALTERNATIVE mechanism for creating overcommit (allow broadly to build depth via accumulated in-flight) — but the action can only raise depth by NOT denying, and at depth-1 there is nothing to deny. Must confirm empirically before committing to any level-based candidate.

(2) Whether the binary per-thread gate has enough authority over the EMERGENT depth at all. The contract says depth is downstream of allowed-issues x in-flight history; but at the depth-1 drain-all operating point the gen-floor result was measured against, the only way the controller raises depth is if denying a thread one tick lets ready accumulate so the next allowed issue coalesces more — a weak, indirect lever. The controller may be unable to reproduce the chunk_floor's depth>1 from the allow gate alone (chunk_floor creates depth by CHUNKING within issue(), a mechanism the gate cannot reach). If so, the controller's realistic role is narrower than 'emulate the gen-floor': it can only REGULATE around whatever depth chunk_floor+D already produce, trimming RSS/variance, not CREATE the depth. This bounds what any candidate can claim.

(3) The win is +3.7% mean / variance-only, and the variance is a 2-4% rare-positive-class tail. Any controller's benefit is therefore SMALL and concentrated in rare windows — measuring it reliably needs many warm-pool seeds (the ADR-0009 min-over-seeds bar, the rare-positive-class problem the offline-label section flags). There is real risk a controller's measured effect is within run-to-run noise; the evaluation must be powered for a tail effect, not a mean effect.

(4) RTT signal quality. The convoy is a metastable TRANSIENT; the RTT EWMA on a slow tick may smooth past the leading edge the latency-aware candidates (C2/C5/C6) rely on. The cadence_ms vs RTT-dynamics separation is both a stability requirement (keep cadence >> RTT) and an observability tension (too slow misses the onset). Where this lands is unknown until RTT is wired and logged.

(5) Surrogate fidelity for C5/C6. server_fed_proxy (producer egress per interval) is the in-process stand-in for server_rows_per_forward, but the server coalesces across ALL producers' messages in ways the per-producer egress does not capture; how well the surrogate tracks the true rows/forward (logged at run exit) is unmeasured and determines whether C5's optimality and C6's L* are calibrated against the right quantity or a biased proxy.
